# Persona Template

*This is a fictional template, not a real person. Copy this file to a new
`<your-slug>.md` (which will be gitignored), fill in real details locally, and
use it with `bin/mask <your-slug>`. Real-people briefs must never be committed
to a public repo — see [README.md](./README.md) for why.*

## Hiring job

What this person is reading the artifact for. One sentence. The decision they
are making in the next 30 minutes.

> Example: "Decide in 60 seconds whether this product solves a problem I'd pay
> to make go away, or whether to close the tab."

## Bounce trigger

What disengages them. Be specific — generic bounce triggers ("vague language",
"too long") produce generic critiques.

> Example: "Pricing page hides the actual numbers behind a 'Contact Sales'
> button."

## Championing trigger

What lights them up. The thing that, if present, makes them want to read more
or share with a colleague. Equally specific.

> Example: "First paragraph names the user's exact pain in their own
> vocabulary, before any product positioning."

## Load-bearing beliefs

3-5 near-quotes from the person's public writing, with citations. These are
the priors they bring. Quote shape matters — exact phrasing carries voice.

> Example:
> - "X is the only metric that compounds." — *Their Blog*, 2026-01
> - "The interesting question isn't Y, it's Z." — *Their Podcast*, 2026-02

## Voice anchors

Stylistic markers — sentence length, vocabulary, rhetorical moves they
repeatedly use. Not "writes well" — describe the actual moves.

> Example:
> - Short declaratives followed by a contrarian "but —"
> - Cites their own prior framings rather than industry consensus
> - Frames problems as economic before technical

## Blindspot

What this person typically misses or underweights, written honestly. Without
an honest blindspot, briefs quietly produce sycophantic critiques. The
blindspot is the most important section to get right.

> Example: "Optimizes for technically literate buyers; tends to dismiss
> products designed for non-technical audiences as 'not interesting' rather
> than 'different audience.'"

## Source corpus

3-5 URLs of the person's public writing, ideally within the last 12 months.
Recent writing carries current priors; older writing may be stale.

> Example:
> - https://example.com/their-blog/2026-04-essay
> - https://example.com/podcast-transcript-2026-02
> - https://example.com/talk-2026-03

## Domain scope

What artifact types this persona is calibrated for. Be honest about the
boundary — when the person says "outside my calibration," the brief should
reflect that.

> Example: "Developer tooling, inference APIs, local-first infrastructure.
> Strong for indie/small-team buyer perspective. Less calibrated for
> enterprise procurement or non-developer products."

## Imitation ceiling note

One sentence. Reproduced verbatim in every output. Reminds the reader the
output is synthetic.

> Example: "Synthetic proxy of <Name>'s priors based on public writings
> through <last_validated>. Stress test, not verdict. Never attribute back to
> them."
